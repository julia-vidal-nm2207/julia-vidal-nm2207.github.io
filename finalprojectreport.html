Online Behavioral Targeting

As we live in an ever more digital world, the process of Online Behavioral Targeting is rapidly gaining importance. This process can be defined as the tracking, processing, and selling user’s data, gathered through their online behavior, for the creation of targeted personalized content (Boerman et al., 2017). This makes OBT highly valuable in the areas of (including but not limited to) marketing and advertising, as it allows for companies to steer their business efforts to only reach relevant audiences. Altogether, OBT allows for a much more optimized and efficient approach to business, which is also highly profitable. However, due to large scale access to sensitive information, OBT has unintended effects, including the manipulation of users and privacy concerns (Barbosa et al.,2021).

The Experiment

This project borrows from the findings I gathered when conducting research on OBT as part of my own bachelor thesis for the University of Amsterdam. The focus of the study was to dive deeper into the concept of OBT transparency mechanisms and the effects they may have in users’ attitudes towards OBT. One of the clearest transparency mechanisms currently available is a profile that summarizes the personality traits that Google identified about each user based on their online surf. If we look at my experiment design, being exposed to this page was my independent variable, and only participants in the experimental group had access to it. I measured attitude towards OBT, my dependent variable, using two indicators: willingness to share data and trust in OBT. For my data story, I am focusing on one mediator used in my study, namely perceived control.

The Data Story

Altogether, with these three variables my data story aims at summarizing the main findings from my bachelor thesis about Online Behavioral Targeting, exposing the real effects that transparency mechanisms have on users. The data story begins by summarizing my sample demographics (age, gender, schooling, and nationality distribution). Then, the main effect is visually portrayed by putting side by side the different attitude levels from the control vs. the experimental group, to put in emphasis the effect that the stimuli had on the sample. Lastly, a network graph to portray the mediation reveals the complex relationship that perceived control, a feeling derived from exposure to the profile, has with the rest of the variables. With this, I aim to expose to my reader the effect of transparency mechanisms on users’ attitude towards OBT, answering the question: What is the effect of transparency mechanisms on user's attitudes towards OBT, and to what extent is this effect mediated by the user's perceived control over their own personal data?

Achievements & Highlights

Revisiting my own thesis and reimagining ways to visualize my down data was very special to me. Learning an entirely new skill that is so far from my comfort zone and being able to incorporate of my biggest interests, which is the world of OBT, showed me just how much knowing basic coding skills can do. I thought it was especially cool to create the bar chart for the main effect. It is such a simple graph, but I had never seen the differences between the two groups of my study so clearly. It is impressive what colors, and a simple, minimalistic chart can do to clearly visualize data. With this simple graph, the main effect of my study had never been so clear. This project also allowed me to dive deeper into the mediation effect of perceived control. In my original study, I looked at more variables (also a moderator) and looking at this but focusing mainly on the moderation allowed for me to look at my results in a completely different way.

Still, I would say my biggest achievement with the construction of this webpage is what I learned with the actual code. I had the chance to filter and apply everything that I learned throughout the course into a project of my own, choosing what pieces of code I thought would fit with my idea and those that did not. In that sense, the entire page is a large achievement, especially considering that I found coding to be a personal challenge. Still, perhaps the thing that made the most difference in my page was the application of the page containers and the dropdown menu that dictates what graph will appear upon selection. Although the entire course is something new that I learned, this is a good example to pick. This added complexity to my webpage makes it more than just a static view, but something that the user can interact with to a certain extent. To do this, I had to not only learn new things that I ‘self-taught’ by looking up online, but also applied concepts from various weeks: week 2 for tags, week 3 for the functions, week 4 for the objects, week 5 for the event listeners, and week 7 and 8 for the graphs and charts.

Out of everything on the webpage though, the thing I had the most fun playing around with was the CSS. Being able to create a webpage that is out of the ordinary and that can look and feel exactly how I wanted it to be was almost as if freeing. It really showed the possibilities of creating almost anything with code, something that with time and practice will become increasingly possible.
