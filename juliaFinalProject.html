Online Behavioral Targeting, the Data Story

The Introduction


What is OBT?

Online Behavioral Targeting has completely revolutionalized modern marketing. With it, organizations can optimize their targeting strategies by tracking users’ online behavior and, with the data gathered, provide them with highly relevant and personalized content. However, with broad access to users’ private data, OBT can also pose serious threats, such as the exploitations of vulnerabilities and privacy concerns.


The study

The experiment conducted by Julia Fernandes Vidal investigates the effect that transparency mechanisms have on how user's feel towards the OBT process. This Data Story will walk you through the main findings.

The Central Question

What is the effect of transparency mechanisms on user's attitudes towards OBT, and to what extent is this effect mediated by the user's perceived control over their own personal data?

The Main Effect

Do transparency mechanisms influence users' attitude towards OBT?

The Treatment

Participants were randomly assigned to one of two conditions: (a) experimental or (b) control. The experimental group had to press a link that took them to their own personal Google page where all their personality and behavioral attributes (as identitified by Goiogle) were listed. They were asked to explore the page and return to the questionnaire once they were done. On the other hand, the control group was given a different but comparable link, one to their Google storage manager, and asked to follow the same instructions. Users' attitudes were then measured.

Measuring Attitude towards OBT

Attitude towards OBT was measured using two indicators: (a) willingness to share data and (b) trust towards OBT. Willingness to share data measured how willing users were to actually share their personal data in order to use free web services. Trust in OBT measured how much users believed their data was kept safe in this transaction.
